## mr_xau — ARTIFACT (disabled)

Root cause: `entry_z = 2.5` is **inside** `stop_z = 3.0`. For a BUY the entry is
`mean + z·sd` with z ≤ −2.5 and the stop is `mean − 3·sd`. The stop is below entry
only when z > −3.0; so **every signal with |z| ≥ 3.0 has its stop on the WRONG side
of entry** (BUY: SL ≥ entry; SELL: SL ≤ entry) and the broker rejects the order.

- The old scratch validators (`mr_validate.py`, `m1_validate.py` — **never committed**)
  booked `stop − entry` on a stop hit with no side check. When the stop is above a
  long's entry, `stop − entry` is **positive**, so a rejected/unfillable order was
  recorded as a **win**. Reproducing that exact code gives **OOS PF 2.74, net +2026**
  — identical to the old `# pf2.74` note, confirming it as the source of the claim.
- Honest run: **54%** of all signals are unfillable and dropped. The tradable
  remainder (2.5 ≤ |z| < 3.0) is a tight-stop fade that loses: **OOS PF 0.72, WR 10%,
  only 1/6 years green, Monte-Carlo 5th-pct PF 0.48**, and it gets worse at higher
  cost. This is not an edge.

Action: slot commented out in `config.yaml` (magic 920623); brain restarted (3 slots
remain). Do **not** re-enable. A corrected design would need the stop placed strictly
beyond the entry per-trade, which is a different, unvalidated strategy.

## zrev_xau — REPRODUCED (keep)

Committed `pipeline/backtest/strategy_zrev.simulate()` (entry_n=100, exit_n=20, cost
0.30) reproduces the edge: **ALL PF 1.48, OOS 1.71, IS 1.25, 5/6 years green, MC
5th-pct 1.10**, stable at double cost. Executability: the live broker SL is the
opposite exit channel (long → exit_dn below entry; short → exit_up above entry) — on
the correct side by construction, **0% invalid**. Backtest vs live entry: backtest
fills at `max(open, channel)` (gap-aware), live sends a market order when the forming
hour breaks the channel — same level ± small slippage already covered by cost. Logic
left untouched (passed external audit).

## orb30_nas — REPRODUCED but THIN (keep, watch)

The live config (DST-aware open + daily SMA50 trend filter + 0.5R breakeven, 1:1) was
**never** in any committed or surviving backtest — the rigorous scratch pass tested
plain ORB at different params. Reproduced here with the **full live logic** on M1
(no-lookahead daily SMA via `.shift(1)`, SL-before-TP, breakeven check order mirroring
`ORBStrategy._exit_hit`): **OOS PF 1.40, IS 1.28, 6/6 years green, MC 5th-pct 1.04,
0% invalid**, survives double cost (1.33). The edge is real but **thin** — expectancy
~0.08R/trade, and the previously-claimed 1.52 was a touch optimistic (real ≈1.40).
Trend filter removes 722 of 1409 breakouts (counter-trend), keeping 687 trades. Keep
at 0.01 lot; do not scale on the strength of this thin margin alone.

## vision_smc_xau — UNVERIFIED by construction (demo only)

A discretionary LLM (Claude vision SMC) cannot be backtested: its decisions depend on

must remain OFF for real money until it has an adequate live track record. Config

## Mean-reversion replacement search — NOTHING PASSED (2026-06-28)

After disabling mr_xau we searched for a *fillable* MR diversifier to replace it.
Code: `research/mr_candidates_research.py`, `research/mr_candidates_b.py` (same
gauntlet). The structural fix for the mr_xau bug: place the stop relative to ENTRY
(ATR-based) so it is always on the correct side -> **0% invalid by construction** in
every candidate below. Then test whether a real edge survives.

Conclusions:
1. Once orders are fillable, **gold H1 mean-reversion has no edge** — proving the old
   mr_xau "profit" was 100% the accounting bug.
2. The only OOS>1 results (RSI-2) **lose in-sample** — the classic "good OOS, bad IS"
   overfit/regime pattern that must be rejected, not deployed. (Booking one of these
   would repeat the mr_xau mistake in a subtler form.)
3. The diversification thesis is real (candidate vs zrev monthly corr ≈ −0.57) but a
   negative-expectancy strategy can't bank it. **No MR slot will be added.**

Decision (user, 2026-06-28): **consolidate** — keep only the reproduced edges
(`zrev_xau`, thin `orb30_nas`), focus on sizing/capitalisation, do not force an MR.

## Take-aways

1. A high PF on a mean-reversion fade with a stop **tighter** than the entry trigger is
   a red flag, not a prize — check order-side validity first.
2. Validation code must be **committed and runnable**, or the number does not exist.
3. "Good OOS, bad IS" is overfit/regime luck — reject it; do not let a low IS slide
   because the recent (OOS) window looks good.
4. Only `zrev_xau` (and, thinly, `orb30_nas`) are reproduced edges. Real-money sizing
   should assume just those two, under-capitalised at $400 (see live-state memory).
